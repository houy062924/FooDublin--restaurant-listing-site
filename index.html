Welcome Foodies!

At the heart of any city, and central to many friendships, food is integral to our daily life. Explore Dublin's rich culinary options through local markets, casual cafe's, or chic restaurants. We want you to be aware of your options and open to exploring new flavours, cultures, and experiences. A perfect way to connect to a place or to another person, we hope you enjoy our handpicked recommendations!

Our Recommendations


A great way to score fresh food, Dublin's food markets give you a vibrant energetic experience. Check out our favourites down below.

Temple Bar

Running on Saturdays from 10-14:30, this market at the centre of Dublin offers great fresh options, rain or shine. Check them out on facebook!

Spencer Dock

A great place to grab a meal for under a tenner, this market at the Art Park offers a delicious selection of meals from food trucks and local chefs every Wednesday from 11:30-14:00.

Smithfield

Lovely fresh and handpicked produce adorns these stalls, so make sure to come early to grab the best fruits and vegetables Ireland can offer! Find them on Cowpinger Row every Friday from 11:00-15:00

If you want a break from city bustle,...

Tang

Just a block away on Dawson Street, this cafe will leave you happy with the delicious food and great coffee all squeezed into a small room.

Bewley's

Open every day of the week on Grafton Street, Bewley's has been a staple of Dublin city centre since it first opened, in 1927! Enjoy a coffee and scone in the remodelled Harry Clarke Gallery, or indulge in one of their many pastries and desserts!

Art Cafe

Just on the North side of town, this small independent cafe will both feed and entertain you with one of their many collabs with local musicians and artists. Step into one of their exhibitions or attend their live music nights, and you'll be sure to have a stellar evening.

If you like the finer things in life, then you'd certainly enjoy some of these higher class options. Definitely worth the price tag.

Chez Max

Red checked tablecloths drape the pavement tables of this classical, Parisian-style bistro. Palace Street.

Chapter One

An elegant style, Michelin starred restaurant, perfect for modern innovative dining, with a traditional Irish twist. Parnell Square.

Pearl Brasserie

Intimate contemporary rooms with peat burning fireplaces for innovative and well presented plates. Merrion Street Upper.

Travel the world from your own city with these lovely tastes of other countries and cultures. Enjoy our favourites below!

Ananda

A stellar Indian restaurant in Dundrum, they will surely impress with their large array of flavours and great tasting menu.

Arisu

This Japanese and Korean fusion menu will definitely delight, so stop through Ranelagh to check them out!

Moro Kitchen

At Moro you will find a delicious mix of North African cuisine, with the owner's own creative twists! Head to Camden Street Lower to try for yourself.
